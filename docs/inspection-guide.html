Step 0: Pre-Walk Setup (15 Minutes Before)

1 Create Simple Zone Map

Draw a basic facility map on whiteboard or paper showing:

  • ENTRANCE (E) - Main entry point
  • THE ROUTE (‚Üí) - Clear inspection path
  • HAZARD ZONE (H) - Roof hole area
  • ELECTRICAL PANEL (P) - Main disconnect
  • CLEARED ZONE (C) - 25% complete area

2 Prepare Welcome Area

  • Set clean table near entrance
  • Lay out hard hats and safety vests
  • Place clipboard with zone map and progress tracker

3 Final Safety Check

  • Sweep designated route - remove tools/debris
  • Ensure hazard area has caution tape and cones
  • Place bucket under drip area
  • Verify 3-foot clearance around electrical panel

Step 1: The Introduction (First 5 Minutes)

Greeting: "Welcome. Thank you for your time. For your safety, please grab a hard hat and vest."
Frame the Tour: "We have a controlled site plan. I've mapped our inspection route here [point to map]. Our process is driven by safety and systematic liquidation, which we track in real-time on this Progress Tracker."
Pro-Tip: Use their name often. Shows preparation and professionalism.

Step 2: The Controlled Walk-Through

Location Action / Script
CLEARED ZONE (C) "This is the outcome of our Phase 1 liquidation. We are systematically turning chaos into capital and achieving a true blank canvas. Our Tracker shows we're 25% complete."
HAZARD ZONE (H) "We have a structural issue here. The area is sealed off. We have [Contractor Name] scheduled, and the permit for repair is being expedited. Safety is our priority until repair is complete."
ELECTRICAL PANEL (P) "This is the main disconnect. We have [Electrician Name/Company], a licensed master electrician, prepared to conduct the necessary safety and repair work. We've isolated the system and cleared the area for immediate access."

Step 4: The Wrap-Up (Exit)

1 Review

"Do you have any immediate concerns regarding our safety protocols or site management, [Inspector's Name]?"

2 Next Step

"What is the next step on your end for the lighting approval, and who should I send the permit package to for the final sign-off?"

3 Follow-Up

Send professional "Thank You" email within one hour:

  • Thank inspector for their time
  • Reiterate commitment to safety
  • Attach electrician contact info
  • Reference next steps discussed
